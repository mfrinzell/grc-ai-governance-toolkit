# Executive & Board-Level GRC Reporting Template

**Purpose:** Structured reporting framework for communicating GRC and AI governance program status to executive leadership and the board  
**Cadence:** Monthly (executive) | Quarterly (steering committee) | Semi-annually (board)

---

## Guiding Principles for Executive Reporting

> Executives and board members are not the audience for operational detail. They need:
> - **So what** — not just what happened, but why it matters
> - **Risk in business terms** — financial, reputational, regulatory exposure
> - **Clear ask** — if you need a decision, say so explicitly
> - **Trend, not snapshot** — is the program improving, holding, or declining?

### 1. Executive Summary (3–5 sentences max)

> Write this last. Summarize the most important thing leadership needs to know this month. Lead with risk posture or a significant development — not activities completed.

*Example: The AI governance program completed risk tiering for all 14 active use cases, with 2 systems identified as Tier 1 (Critical). Both are now in remediation with a target completion date of [DATE]. No regulatory findings were received this period. Program health remains GREEN.*

## Template 3: Board / Audit Committee Reporting (Semi-Annual)

**Format:** 1–2 page narrative + 1 dashboard page | Written brief preferred over slides

### Section 1: Risk Posture Statement (1 paragraph)
State the organization's current GRC and AI governance risk posture. Is risk increasing, decreasing, or stable? What are the primary drivers?

### Section 2: Material Risks
List the top 3–5 risks by business impact. For each:
- Risk description (plain language)
- Potential business impact (financial, regulatory, reputational)
- Current mitigation status
- Residual risk level

### Section 3: Regulatory Exposure
- Summary of applicable laws and regulations (AI Act, state privacy laws, sector-specific rules)
- Compliance status vs. key requirements
- Any enforcement actions or material regulatory changes

### Section 4: Program Performance (Scorecard)
One-page dashboard showing KRI trends and program health across all workstreams.

### Section 5: Significant Incidents
Any material incidents during the period — summary, response, and resolution status.

### Section 6: Looking Ahead
Top priorities for the next reporting period. Any resource or strategic asks.

## Executive Communication Tips

**Do:**
- Lead with the bottom line — executives read the first sentence most carefully
- Use consistent RAG (Red/Amber/Green) status across all reports
- Quantify impact in dollars, customers, or regulatory exposure when possible
- Distinguish between "risk accepted" and "risk in remediation"
- Pre-brief your executive sponsor before distributing widely

**Don't:**
- List activities without linking to outcomes or risk reduction
- Bury bad news — surface it early with a clear path forward
- Use jargon without definition (especially in board materials)
- Report the same metric differently across different reports
- Wait for a formal report cycle to escalate urgent risks
